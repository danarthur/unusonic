# Employee Portal Benchmark Report

Research date: 2026-04-02

---

## 1. Category A: Event Production / Staffing Platforms

### LASSO (lasso.io) -- Market Leader for Live Events

**Portal sections:** Schedule (calendar view color-coded by employer), Job Opportunities, Messages, Travel Itineraries, Time Tracking, Availability.

**Schedule view:** Calendar-based, color-coded by employer. Employees see multi-employer schedules in a single view -- critical for freelancers working multiple companies.

**Profile:** Skills, certifications, contact info, rates, contracts stored centrally. Admin-controlled rate structures.

**Pay:** Payroll integration via timesheet export. Complex pay rules (OT, travel days, per diem) automated. Employees track hours via mobile clock-in.

**Dashboard/Home:** Job opportunities queue, upcoming schedule, unread messages. Travel itineraries front and center.

**Notifications:** Push, SMS, and email for new job invitations, schedule changes, travel detail updates.

**Documents:** Digital new-hire paperwork, mobile signatures, ongoing policy changes. W-9/I-9 handled at onboarding.

**Communication:** Direct mobile messaging for changing event/travel details.

**Time tracking:** App-based clock-in/out. Supervisors can clock in crew on their behalf. Flexible time system with industry-specific pay rules.

**Mobile:** Native app + mobile-responsive web fallback + SMS/email for non-app users. Offline access for spotty venue connectivity. Dark mode for on-location use.

**Key differentiator:** Multi-employer calendar view. Travel itinerary integration. Offline mode. The fact that crew can work for multiple companies and see everything in one view is a pattern no general HR tool offers.

---

### Nowsta (nowsta.com) -- Event Staffing Powerhouse

**Portal sections:** Job Requests, Calendar/Schedule, Earnings, Timeclock, Notifications.

**Schedule view:** Calendar view in the worker app. Employees accept/decline shifts directly, instantly updating the master schedule. Shift pool concept ("make your own schedule").

**Profile:** Worker profiles with skills, uniform sizes, certifications.

**Pay:** Earnings tab showing estimated pay per shift. Earnings tracker visible before accepting a shift.

**Dashboard/Home:** Incoming job requests, upcoming shifts, announcements/updates.

**Notifications:** Push notifications + SMS for shift requests. GPS-enabled reminders when approaching venue.

**Documents:** Not extensively documented publicly.

**Time tracking:** GPS self check-in captures location to verify staff are at the event. Time and attendance dashboard with PDF timesheet generation. SMS reminders for clock-in.

**Mobile:** Mobile-first design. Accept/decline directly from push notification.

**Key differentiator:** Demand forecasting. Reusable scheduling templates. The "accept from push notification" pattern -- one tap to confirm a shift without opening the app -- dramatically reduces friction.

---

### StaffConnect (staffconnect-app.com) -- Agency-Focused

**Portal sections:** Profile, Job Board, Schedule, GPS Check-in/out, Timesheets, Payments, Quizzes/Training.

**Schedule view:** Calendar with job listings. Staff receive automated recommendations based on role, availability, location, past performance.

**Profile:** Custom data collection from candidates. Digital contract signing via PDF upload.

**Pay:** One-click timesheet generation. Individual talent invoices. Payroll export.

**Notifications:** Push notifications, automatic reminders, live chat, communications hub.

**Documents:** Custom quizzes for health questionnaires, product knowledge. Digital contracts.

**Time tracking:** GPS Check-in/out with no-show alerts.

**Key differentiator:** AI-powered talent recommendations for events. Custom quiz/training modules pushed to staff.

---

### Liveforce (liveforce.co) -- Event Staffing

**Portal sections (Crew App):** Profile, Work Calendar, Job Board, Timesheets, Expenses, Payments.

**Schedule view:** Calendar with availability management built in. Availability info feeds scheduling decisions.

**Profile:** Staff-managed profiles with agency-controlled fields.

**Pay:** Track payments per job. Submit expenses directly from app.

**Mobile:** Native iOS/Android + browser access. Push notifications for updates.

**Key differentiator:** White-label capability -- agencies brand the app as their own. Expense submission from the field.

---

### Rentman (rentman.io) -- AV/Production Rental + Crew

**Portal sections:** Schedule, Job Invitations, Call Sheets, Worked Hours Log, Availability.

**Schedule view:** Job-based view showing assigned projects. Accept/decline invitations. View call sheets and job details.

**Profile:** Skills, certifications, contact info. Managed by admin with crew input.

**Pay:** Logged hours tracked per job. Cost tracking in real-time.

**Pricing model:** Unlimited free crew accounts. Only planners/managers pay. This removes all friction from onboarding freelancers.

**Key differentiator:** Free crew accounts with no limit. Call sheet distribution built into the workflow. Designed for the rental + crew combo that AV/production companies need.

---

### Wrapbook (wrapbook.com) -- Production Payroll

**Portal sections:** Jobs, Startwork (onboarding), Timecards, Expenses, Pay History, Tax Documents.

**Profile:** Persistent crew profiles that follow workers job-to-job. Autofill startwork from previous jobs.

**Pay:** Full pay history across all Wrapbook jobs. Pay stubs, payment status tracking, tax documents (W-2, 1099). Complex union/non-union payroll calculations.

**Time tracking:** Smart mobile timecards with mileage, kit fees, allowances. Union rule calculations built in.

**Key differentiator:** Profile persistence across productions. "Fill out paperwork once" model. Complex entertainment payroll rules (union rates, overtime tiers, meal penalties) handled automatically.

---

### Shoflo (shoflo.tv) -- Run-of-Show Only

Not a full employee portal. Focused on real-time cue sheet/rundown collaboration. Four permission levels (read-only, contributor, admin, show caller). Guest Pass links for vendors/crew who don't need accounts. Relevant to Unusonic's Plan tab but not the employee portal.

---

### Prismm/AllSeated -- Venue Design Only

Acquired by Cvent in 2025. Floor planning and 3D venue visualization. No crew portal features. Not relevant to employee portal.

---

### IntelliEvent -- AV/Production Operations

Labor scheduling with global resource view. Call sheet generation. 200+ management reports. Portal for viewing/editing event details. Less crew-self-service focused, more manager-centric.

## 2. Category B: Best-in-Class General Employee Portals

### Gusto (gusto.com) -- Gold Standard for Small Business

**Portal sections:** Dashboard, Pay (stubs + history), Benefits, Time Off, Tax Documents (W-2/1099), Profile, Time Clock, Financial Tools (Gusto Wallet).

**Dashboard:** Pay period summary, upcoming pay date, PTO balance, action items (open enrollment, missing info).

**Profile:** Employee-editable: address, direct deposit, tax withholdings, emergency contacts. Admin-controlled: title, department, compensation.

**Pay:** Detailed pay stub breakdown (gross, deductions by type, taxes by jurisdiction, net). Full pay history. Year-to-date totals.

**Tax documents:** W-2 and 1099 download without asking anyone.

**Time tracking:** Clock in/out, timesheet submission.

**Financial wellness:** Gusto Wallet -- early wage access, fee-free debit card, budgeting tools, savings accounts. This is increasingly table-stakes.

**Key differentiator:** The pay stub detail level -- employees understand exactly where every dollar went. Gusto Wallet as a retention/engagement tool.

---

### Rippling (rippling.com) -- Enterprise-Grade All-in-One

**Portal sections:** Home/Dashboard, Pay (stubs, 1099s), Benefits, Time Off, Expenses, Directory/Contacts.

**Pay:** Paystubs, direct deposit management, tax documents.

**Profile:** Personal information updates with approval workflows where needed.

**Known UX issue:** Users report frequent sign-outs and the app redirecting to a separate browser for login -- a broken pattern that kills mobile adoption.

**Key differentiator:** Unified HR + IT + Finance in one platform. But the employee-facing UX is secondary to the admin experience.

---

### BambooHR (bamboohr.com) -- Mid-Market HR

**Portal sections:** Home, My Info, People (directory), Files, Time Off, Pay, Benefits.

**Dashboard:** Upcoming time off, announcements, birthday/anniversary celebrations, who's out today.

**Profile:** Tabs for personal info, job info, employment history, documents, assets, benefits, time off.

**Pay:** Pay stubs with full breakdown. Tax form access.

**Benefits:** Self-service enrollment during open enrollment or qualifying life events.

**Key differentiator:** People directory with org chart. The "who's out today" feature is simple but high-engagement. Former employee self-service access (recent addition) -- lets ex-employees pull their own tax docs.

---

### Justworks (justworks.com) -- PEO Model

**Portal sections:** Dashboard, Pay, Benefits, Time Off, Time Tracking, Documents, Profile.

**Dashboard:** Upcoming time off, recent pay, benefits summary, action items.

**Time Off:** Request flow with balance visibility, approval status, company holiday calendar.

**Mobile:** iOS/Android app mirroring web portal. Paystubs, benefits, time off, hour tracking all mobile-accessible.

---

### Deputy (deputy.com) -- Shift Management Excellence

**Schedule view:** Two views on mobile -- Day View (who's working, where, when) and List View (chronological shifts). The "Me" tab shows personal upcoming shifts + calendar display for the week.

**Calendar sync:** iOS calendar subscription auto-syncs. Google, Outlook, Apple calendar integration with automatic updates when Deputy schedule changes. This is a must-have.

**Time tracking:** Clock in/out from Android, iOS, or Apple Watch. Geofencing support.

**Availability:** Visual availability management. Easy for managers to see when people are/aren't available.

**Key differentiator:** Calendar subscription sync is the killer feature. Employees see Deputy shifts in their personal calendar without any manual effort. Schedule changes propagate automatically.

---

### When I Work (wheniwork.com) -- Team Scheduling

**Schedule view:** Shift-based view with swap/drop functionality.

**Communication:** 1:1 and group chat. No personal phone numbers needed -- all communication through the app.

**Time tracking:** GPS clock-in with geofencing.

**Shift management:** Availability management, time-off requests, collaborative shift trades with manager approval workflow.

**Key differentiator:** The "no personal phone numbers" communication model -- employees interact through the platform, preserving privacy.

---

### 7shifts (7shifts.com) -- Restaurant-Specific (Adjacent Industry)

**Dashboard:** Shift Pool showing available shifts to bid on. Drop/trade/pick up extra shifts.

**Schedule view:** Compact mobile view optimized for at-a-glance access.

**Profile:** Wage-based role info shown prominently -- employees see their rate per role immediately.

**Pay:** Hours + estimated earnings visible. Tip reporting (employer-configurable).

**Communication:** GIF/photo chat. Quick-contact to available managers by department.

**Key differentiator:** Shift Pool is a marketplace pattern -- open shifts are "published" and staff bid/claim them. This is highly engaging and gives employees agency. Rate-per-role visibility prevents confusion when workers fill multiple roles.

---

### Homebase (joinhomebase.com) -- Small Business

**Schedule view:** Template-based scheduling with AI assistant.

**Communication:** Multi-location company-wide channels + department-specific chat rooms (e.g., "servers chat," "kitchen chat").

**Documents:** Digital onboarding, document collection, applicant tracking.

**Pay:** Integrated payroll from timesheets. Labor law compliance monitoring with automatic alerts.

**Key differentiator:** Department-scoped chat rooms -- relevant messages reach relevant people only. Overtime approach alerts before it happens.

---

### Connecteam (connecteam.com) -- All-in-One Frontline

**Portal sections:** Time Clock, Schedule, Chat, Forms/Checklists, Training/Documents, Directory.

**Time tracking:** GPS + geofence verification. Job/shift attachments. Automated break/OT calculations. Push notification reminders.

**Communication:** Team chat with images, videos, files, GIFs, location sharing. Pinned conversations. Scheduled messages during work hours only.

**Forms:** Custom digital forms and checklists completed in real-time from the field.

**Training:** Mobile courses, quizzes, onboarding content pushed to phones. Completion tracking.

**Key differentiator:** The "forms and checklists" pattern is powerful for event production -- think safety inspections, load-in checklists, equipment checks. Training modules push product knowledge to crew before an event.

## 3. Category C: Gig/Shift Worker Platforms

### Instawork (instawork.com)

**Job finding:** Filter by distance, pay rate, role type, start time. Each listing shows pay, expectations, attire, timing, check-in details.

**Profile:** 30+ skill data points. Work history, skill quizzes, references, certifications, headshots, ratings.

**Pay:** Pay details visible before accepting each shift. Earnings tab with complete payment history. Weekly pay cycle (Mon-Sun, processed Wed). "Instapay" for instant post-shift payment at higher tiers.

**Gamification:** Top Pro Program with Silver/Gold/Platinum levels. Badges unlock new job categories. Priority shift access based on performance tier.

**Key differentiator:** The tiered rewards program drives quality and retention. Showing exact pay before shift acceptance is now expected by gig workers. Instant pay is a competitive requirement.

---

### Wonolo (wonolo.com)

**Profile:** Skills-based with badge system. "Wonolo U" training platform for earning certificates.

**Pay:** Direct deposit via Stripe. 98% of jobs paid within 2 business days. Flat rate or hourly shown before claiming.

**Schedule:** Self-serve shift claiming from available pool. First-come-first-served for popular slots.

**Gamification:** Badge system for skill progression. Badges unlock access to new job categories.

**Key differentiator:** Badge/certification progression as unlock mechanism. Training integrated into the earning path.

## 4. Cross-Cutting Analysis

### A. What Every Good Employee Portal Has (Table Stakes)

| Feature | Status for Unusonic |
|---|---|
| Schedule/upcoming shifts | Have route, needs build-out |
| Profile with editable personal info | Have route, needs build-out |
| Pay stubs / earnings history | Have route, needs build-out |
| Push notifications for schedule changes | Not started |
| Calendar sync (Google/Apple/Outlook) | Not started |
| Time clock / check-in | Not started |
| Document storage (tax forms, certs) | Not started |
| Mobile-optimized layout | Partially (responsive shell exists) |

### B. What the Best Tools Do That Others Don't

1. **Multi-employer calendar view (LASSO)** -- Freelancers see all their work across companies in one calendar. Unusonic's model (employees belong to one workspace) doesn't need this yet, but crew who freelance for multiple production companies would value it eventually.

2. **Shift marketplace / Shift Pool (7shifts, Instawork, Wonolo)** -- Open shifts published as a pool that workers claim. Creates agency and engagement. For event production: "We need 3 more stagehands for Saturday" published to the crew pool.

3. **One-tap accept from push notification (Nowsta)** -- Confirming a shift without opening the app. Reduces response time from hours to seconds.

4. **Calendar subscription sync (Deputy)** -- Portal shifts automatically appear in personal Google/Apple/Outlook calendars. Updates propagate without employee action. This is the single most impactful feature for portal adoption.

5. **Profile persistence across jobs (Wrapbook)** -- Fill out paperwork once, reuse across productions. Crew profiles follow workers.

6. **GPS geofence check-in (Nowsta, Deputy, NextCrew)** -- Automatic or verified clock-in when physically at the venue. Eliminates buddy punching and manual time entry.

7. **Pre-shift info cards (Instawork, Nowsta)** -- Before accepting: exact pay, location, dress code, parking info, check-in instructions. Everything needed to decide and prepare, in one card.

8. **Performance-based tiering (Instawork, Wonolo)** -- Badges, ratings, tier progression that unlocks better shifts, faster pay, priority access. Drives quality and retention.

9. **Offline mode (LASSO)** -- Event venues have notoriously bad connectivity. Offline access to schedule, call sheets, and contact info is production-specific and critical.

10. **Department-scoped chat (Homebase, 7shifts)** -- Messages routed to relevant people only. "Lighting crew" channel vs "catering crew" channel. Prevents noise.

### C. What's Still Broken Across All Tools

1. **Fragmented ecosystem.** Event crew commonly use 3-5 apps: one for scheduling, one for payroll, one for communication, one for timesheets, one for documents. No single tool owns the full crew experience in production.

2. **No unified freelancer identity.** A freelance stagehand working for 4 production companies has 4 separate profiles, 4 separate apps, 4 separate login credentials. Nobody owns the "freelancer passport" for live events.

3. **Pay transparency is inconsistent.** Some tools show estimated earnings, others don't. Workers frequently can't see rate breakdowns (base + OT + per diem + travel + kit fee) until after payroll processes.

4. **Notification fatigue vs. notification blindness.** Too many platforms sending too many push notifications. Workers either mute everything or miss critical schedule changes.

5. **Onboarding paperwork is still painful.** Despite digital signatures, most platforms require re-entering the same info for each new company/production. W-9, direct deposit, emergency contact -- repeated endlessly.

6. **No connection between schedule and run-of-show.** Schedule tools show "you work 8am-6pm on Saturday." They don't show "at 9:15am you're on stage for the keynote changeover, at 11:00am you're at dock B for the breakout room load-in." The gap between "when you work" and "what you do minute-by-minute" is universal.

7. **Calendar sync is an afterthought.** Most tools offer it but implement it poorly -- stale data, wrong timezone handling, no location info in the calendar event.

8. **Rate visibility for multi-role workers.** Crew often fill different roles (stagehand vs. rigger vs. crew chief) at different rates. Most tools show a single rate, not per-role breakdowns.

### D. Patterns from Adjacent Industries Worth Borrowing

| Pattern | Source Industry | Application for Unusonic |
|---|---|---|
| **Shift Pool marketplace** | Restaurant (7shifts), Gig (Instawork) | Publish open crew positions to qualified crew. Self-service claiming with manager approval. |
| **Performance tiers with perks** | Gig economy (Instawork, Wonolo) | Preferred freelancers get first access to shows, faster pay, higher visibility. Aligns with existing `tier: 'preferred'` in Ghost Protocol. |
| **Digital checklists from field** | Frontline ops (Connecteam) | Pre-show safety checks, equipment inventories, load-in checklists submitted from the venue floor. |
| **Training modules pushed to mobile** | Frontline ops (Connecteam), Gig (Wonolo) | Push product knowledge, safety certs, venue-specific orientation before the show date. |
| **Scheduled/scoped messaging** | Restaurant (Homebase, 7shifts) | Show-specific or department-specific channels. "Messages for the Friday load-in crew only." |
| **Early wage access** | Payroll (Gusto Wallet) | Earned wage access before payroll cycle. Competitive advantage for attracting freelance crew. |
| **Smart reminders at 24h and 2h** | Shift management (Myshyft research) | Automated reminders reduce no-shows by 30-40%. Two-touch: 24 hours and 2 hours before call time. |
| **Calendar subscription (not just export)** | Shift management (Deputy) | Live-updating iCal feed. Changes in Unusonic automatically appear in crew's personal calendars. |

### E. What Makes Employees Actually Use a Portal vs. Ignore It

Research consistently identifies these factors:

1. **Solve a real pain, not a theoretical one.** "See my next shift" and "see my pay" are the two things that drive first login. Everything else comes after.

2. **Under 3 taps to core info.** Schedule and pay must be accessible within 3 taps from app open. Portals requiring login, navigation, and drilling down see 45-65% adoption. Streamlined ones see 85-95%.

3. **Push notifications that respect boundaries.** 1-2 non-urgent notifications per day maximum. Urgent (schedule change) delivered immediately via push + SMS. Never wake people up for non-critical messages.

4. **The portal must be the ONLY way to do something.** If managers also text schedules via iMessage, employees will ignore the portal. Shift acceptance, time-off requests, and pay stubs must ONLY live in the portal.

5. **Speed.** If the portal takes more than 2 seconds to show the schedule, employees will check their calendar app instead. Performance is a feature.

6. **Don't require the app.** The best platforms (LASSO, Rentman) work via mobile web, native app, and fall back to SMS/email for non-app users. Forcing an app download is a barrier.

7. **Personalization.** Show only what's relevant to the employee's role and department. Information overload is the top complaint.

## 5. Recommended Portal Sections for Unusonic

Based on the research, the portal should have these sections in priority order:

### Tier 1 -- Ship First (drives adoption)

| Section | Content | Benchmark Reference |
|---|---|---|
| **Home/Dashboard** | Next upcoming show (date, venue, role, call time). Unread notifications count. Quick link to full schedule. | 7shifts dashboard, Gusto home |
| **Schedule** | List view (default, mobile-optimized) of upcoming shows with date, venue, role, times. Tap to expand into show detail card (call time, venue address, parking, dress code, crew list, run-of-show link). | Nowsta calendar, LASSO schedule |
| **Pay** | Earnings history by show. Rate breakdown per show (base hours x rate + OT + per diem + travel + kit fees). Current pay period summary. | Gusto pay stubs, Wrapbook pay history |

### Tier 2 -- Ship Next (reduces admin burden)

| Section | Content | Benchmark Reference |
|---|---|---|
| **Profile** | Personal info (employee-editable: phone, address, emergency contact, direct deposit). Skills/certifications (admin-managed). Photo. | BambooHR My Info, Instawork profile |
| **Documents** | Tax forms (W-9, 1099). Insurance certificates. Certifications with expiry tracking. Digital signatures on contracts. | Gusto tax docs, Workforce.com certifications |
| **Notifications** | Notification center with categories: schedule, pay, documents, messages. Preference controls for push/email/SMS per category. | Myshyft research, Nowsta notifications |

### Tier 3 -- Ship Later (engagement + differentiation)

| Section | Content | Benchmark Reference |
|---|---|---|
| **Check-in/out** | GPS-verified clock-in at venue. Break tracking. Timesheet auto-generation. | Nowsta GPS check-in, Deputy geofence |
| **Calendar sync** | iCal subscription feed. Auto-updating Google/Apple/Outlook integration. | Deputy calendar sync |
| **Messages** | Show-specific channels. Direct messages to crew lead / production manager. No personal phone numbers exchanged. | When I Work chat, Homebase channels |
| **Shift Pool** | Available open positions crew can claim (requires manager approval). | 7shifts Shift Pool, Instawork |
| **Training** | Pre-show briefings, safety modules, venue orientation pushed before call date. | Connecteam training, Wonolo U |

## 6. Unusonic's Current State vs. Benchmark

The portal currently has three routes (`/schedule`, `/profile`, `/pay`) with a top-bar shell. Based on the benchmark:

**What's right:**
- The three core sections match what every platform starts with
- Role-gated routing (employees to portal, others to dashboard) matches Deputy/Rippling patterns
- The shell is minimal and mobile-friendly

**What's missing (highest impact first):**
1. No dashboard/home page -- employees land on... schedule? There should be a home with "your next show" front and center
2. No notification system -- this is the #1 driver of portal engagement
3. No calendar sync -- Deputy proves this is the single highest-value feature for adoption
4. No document management -- tax forms and certifications are a constant admin pain point
5. No show detail cards -- the schedule should expand into rich pre-shift info (venue, parking, dress code, crew list, ROS link)
6. No check-in/check-out -- time tracking is table stakes for production companies
7. No messaging -- crew currently coordinate via personal text, WhatsApp, or Slack

**Unusonic's structural advantage:**
The knowledge graph architecture (entities + cortex relationships) means Unusonic can connect schedule to run-of-show to crew to venue to pay in a way that fragmented tools cannot. The "what you do minute-by-minute" gap identified in Section 4C.6 is something Unusonic is uniquely positioned to close because the Plan tab's run-of-show data and the portal's schedule view share the same data layer.
